Places of attraction

Moremi Game Reserve

Moremi Game Reserve is a protected wildlife area in the Okavango Delta known for its rich biodiversity. It combines forests, floodplains, lagoons, and savannah, supporting large populations of elephants, lions, leopards, buffalo, and many bird species. It is considered one of the best places in Africa for game viewing because of its mix of habitats.

Rail Transport

Botswana also has railway services mainly used for cargo transport, although passenger trains operate on selected routes. Rail travel is less commonly used by tourists compared to buses and flights.

Best Time To Visit

Botswana experiences different seasons throughout the year, and each season offers unique travel experiences. The ideal time to visit depends on the activities travelers wish to enjoy.

Dry Season (May to October)

The dry season is considered the best time to visit Botswana. During these months, temperatures are cooler and rainfall is very limited. Wildlife viewing becomes easier because animals gather near rivers, waterholes, and permanent water sources.

Advantages of Visiting During the Dry Season

  • Excellent wildlife viewing opportunities
  • Comfortable daytime temperatures
  • Lower chances of rain disrupting activities
  • Ideal conditions for safaris and camping

June to August are the coolest months, especially during early mornings and evenings. Tourists are advised to carry warm clothing because temperatures can drop significantly at night.

Hot Season (September to October)

These months are extremely hot, particularly in northern Botswana. However, they are still excellent for game viewing because animals remain concentrated around water sources. Visitors should drink plenty of water and avoid prolonged exposure to the sun.

Rainy Season (November to April)

The rainy season transforms Botswana into a green and beautiful landscape. Although some roads become muddy or difficult to use, this season is popular among bird watchers because migratory birds arrive during this time.

Advantages of the Rainy Season

  • Beautiful green scenery
  • Fewer tourists and quieter attractions
  • Best season for bird watching
  • Lower accommodation costs in some areas

Thunderstorms may occur in the afternoons, but rainfall is usually short and followed by sunshine.

Best Time for Specific Activities

  • Safari and wildlife viewing: May to October
  • Bird watching: November to April
  • Photography: Green season for landscapes, dry season for wildlife
  • Cultural tourism: Any time of the year

Safety & Guideliness

Botswana is known for being politically stable and relatively safe for travelers. However, visitors should still follow safety measures and respect local customs to ensure a comfortable and enjoyable stay.

Personal Safety

Travelers should keep valuables such as phones, passports, and money secure at all times. It is advisable to avoid displaying expensive jewelry or carrying large amounts of cash openly. Walking alone at night in isolated areas should also be avoided.

Road Safety

Road travel is generally safe, but drivers should be cautious of animals crossing roads, especially in rural areas and near national parks. Driving at night outside cities is discouraged because visibility may be poor and wildlife accidents are common.

Important Road Guidelines
  • Wear seatbelts at all times
  • Follow speed limits carefully
  • Avoid using mobile phones while driving
  • Carry emergency supplies for long trips
Health Precautions

Travelers should drink clean water and protect themselves from heat and dehydration, especially during summer months. Mosquito repellent is recommended when visiting northern regions where mosquitoes are more common. Travel insurance is also highly recommended.

Wildlife Safety

Botswana is famous for its wildlife, but animals in parks and reserves can be dangerous if approached carelessly. Visitors should always follow instructions from guides and rangers.

Wildlife Safety Rules
  • Never feed wild animals
  • Stay inside vehicles during safaris unless instructed otherwise
  • Keep a safe distance from animals
  • Avoid making loud noises near wildlife
Cultural Guidelines

Botswana people are generally respectful and welcoming. Visitors are encouraged to behave politely, greet people respectfully, and dress modestly in public places. Asking permission before taking photographs of local people is considered respectful.

Emergency Information

Visitors should keep emergency contacts and important documents easily accessible. It is also advisable to know the location of nearby hospitals, police stations, and embassies while traveling.
